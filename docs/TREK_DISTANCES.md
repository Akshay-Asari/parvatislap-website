### **6. Pin Parvati Pass Trek** üèîÔ∏è (Most Challenging)

**Approximate Coordinates:** 32.200¬∞N, 77.500¬∞E  
**Starting Point:** Barshaini ‚Üí Ending Point:** Mudh (Spiti Valley)

- **Linear Distance:** ~25 km from Parvati's Lap
- **Total Trekking Distance:** 90-110 km
- **Duration:** 10-12 days
- **Difficulty:** Extremely Difficult (Technical)
- **Pass Altitude:** 5,319m (17,451 ft)
- **Best Season:** July to September only

**Trek Itinerary:**
- **Day 1:** Barshaini ‚Üí Kheerganga (12 km)
- **Day 2:** Kheerganga ‚Üí Tunda Bhuj (8 km)
- **Day 3:** Tunda Bhuj ‚Üí Thakur Kuan (7 km)
- **Day 4:** Thakur Kuan ‚Üí Odi Thach (8 km)
- **Day 5:** Odi Thach ‚Üí Mantalai (7 km)
- **Day 6:** Acclimatization Day at Mantalai
- **Day 7:** Mantalai ‚Üí Pin Parvati Base Camp (5 km)
- **Day 8:** Base Camp ‚Üí Cross Pass ‚Üí Mantalai Camp (Spiti side) (12 km, 10-12 hrs)
- **Day 9-12:** Descent through Spiti Valley to Mudh

**Key Highlights:**
- Highest trekking pass in Parvati region (5,319m)
- Connects green Parvati Valley with cold Spiti desert
- Cross glaciers, moraines, and boulder fields
- Mantalai Lake (sacred to locals)
- Technical sections requiring ropes and ice axes
- Spectacular views of Kullu and Spiti ranges

**Requirements:**
- High-altitude trekking experience mandatory
- Technical gear (crampons, ice axe, rope)
- Professional guide essential
- Medical fitness certificate required
- Travel insurance recommended

**Description:**  
One of the most challenging and adventurous treks in the Indian Himalayas. This epic trans-Himalayan journey connects the lush, green Parvati Valley with the stark, cold desert of Spiti Valley. Cross the formidable Pin Parvati Pass at 5,319m, traverse glaciers, navigate moraines, and experience the dramatic landscape transformation from verdant forests to barren high-altitude terrain. Only for experienced, fit trekkers seeking the ultimate Himalayan challenge.

## üìå Important Notes

### **Best Trekking Season:**
- **Summer (April-June):** Clear views, snow at higher altitudes, wildflowers bloom
- **Monsoon (July-August):** Avoid - slippery trails, leeches, landslide risk
- **Autumn (September-November):** Clear skies, pleasant weather, best for photography
- **Winter (December-March):** Heavy snow, most treks closed except for experienced winter trekkers

### **Essential Tips:**
1. **Acclimatization:** Take rest days for high-altitude treks
2. **Permits:** Check if forest department permits are required
3. **Guide:** Hire local guides for offbeat/difficult treks
4. **Fitness:** Maintain good cardiovascular fitness
5. **Gear:** Proper trekking boots, warm clothes, rain protection
6. **Leave No Trace:** Carry all waste back, respect nature
